Ashtanga

One important concept of classical yoga is the Ashtanga--The 8 limbs of yoga.

  1. Yama

    2. Yamas are universal practices that relate best to what we know as the Golden Rule, "Do unto others as you would have them do unto you.

  2. Niyama

    3. It deals with self-discipline and spiritual observances.

  3. Asana

    4. The postures practiced in yoga, comprise the third limb.

  4. Pranayama

    5. Generally translated as breath control, this fourth stage consists of techniques designed to gain mastery over the respiratory process while recognizing the connection between the breath, the mind, and the emotions.

  5. Pratyahara

    6. It means withdrawal or sensory transcendence.

  6. Dharana

    7. The practice of pratyahara creates the setting for dharana, or concentration.

  7. Dhyana

    8. It is the uninterrupted flow of concentration.

  8. Samadhi

    9. At this stage, the meditator merges with his or her point of focus and transcends the Self altogether.
